The Conservation Results Chain Ontology provides a formal, machine-readable vocabulary for representing Theories of Change in the domain of biodiversity conservation. Based on the widely adopted framework by Margoluis et al. (2013), this ontology connects conservation actions to their intermediate results, threat reductions, and ultimate impacts. By semantically integrating standard classifications—such as the Conservation Measures Partnership (CMP) Actions and Threats, Conservation Evidence, and UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)—this ontology enables interoperability, standardized monitoring, and evidence-based planning across diverse conservation projects.
